# Resume Writing Rules

## Content Accuracy

- NEVER fabricate experience, skills, or achievements — only use what exists in `data/`
- Rewording for relevance is encouraged; inventing is forbidden
- If the user lacks a required skill, flag it honestly rather than faking it

## Tailoring Strategy

- Mirror the job posting's language and keywords naturally in bullet points
- Lead with the most relevant experience — not necessarily the most recent
- Prioritize quantified achievements (revenue, %, time saved, team size) over generic duties
- Match the tone to the industry (e.g., corporate for finance, conversational for startups)

## Bullet Point Quality

- Start every bullet with a strong action verb (Led, Built, Reduced, Designed, etc.)
- Follow the pattern: Action + Context + Result/Impact
- Keep bullets to 1-2 lines; cut filler words aggressively
- Avoid buzzwords without substance ("synergy", "leveraged", "spearheaded" with no metric)

## Formatting

- Keep the resume to 1-2 pages depending on experience level
- Use consistent date formatting (e.g., Jan 2020 – Mar 2023)
- Most recent experience first within each section (reverse chronological)
- Only include a summary/objective if it adds real value for the specific role

## ATS Optimization

- Include exact keywords from the job posting where truthful
- Avoid tables, columns, headers/footers, and images that ATS parsers struggle with
- Use standard section headings: Experience, Education, Skills, Certifications
